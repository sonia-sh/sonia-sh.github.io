Clover - Smart Home Management App

UI/UX Design

My Role: UI Designer

Team Members: Sonia, Vivian

Tools: AdobeXD

Skills: User Research, User Personas, Wireframing, Prototyping

Project Background

This project was made for the Adobe x Ring Creative Jam that ran in June 2021. We wentered as a team of two students and had one week to prepare an AdobeXD prototype of our proposed mobile app.

The Challenge

The competition's challenge was to design an "inclusive third-party mobile app". There was a focus on creating something in line with Ring's devices, to "improve surveillance, communication, temperature, and entertainment around the home and neighbourhood".

What Is Clover?

The state of a home often reflects the state of the person living there. Without a solid cleaning and maintenance routine, it is easy for people to slip into a disarray.

Clover is for those who wish to indirectly keep updated on their "partner's" everyday routine in a non-intrusice manner, or those looking to keep themselves accountable with household cleaning, excellent for carers and roommates respectively.

It combines this social feature with the ability to track and monitor selected smart appliances, allowing for a streamlined experience both on the app and in the real world.

The app has a green, white, and blue palette to communicate a clean atmosphere. This helps users less familiar with technology adapt to our interface. We want to help users build real, strong habits for their long-term benefit.

Who Is Clover For?

For people who have someone they care about, but don't have continual contact or access to (e.g., remote, living alone, busy schedules or different timezones).

The app offers a way to indirectly keep updated on their "partner's" day to day routine in a non-intrusive manner (e.g., Parent-Child, Partners, Close Friends).

For people who may live with someone and share the same appliances, and would like to have better insights into usage.

The app offers a way to indirectly keep updated on appliance usage, status, etc., and understand lifestyle habits of another person (e.g., roommates).
